## TL;DR

- **Stick with Hetzner** when you need `€5.19/mo`, predictable European latency, and stable billing.
- **Oracle Always Free** wins for one-off bots or testing—for production add swap and expect ARM pitfalls.
- **Vultr & Scaleway** are good when you need US or EU nodes with similar pricing but more flexible billing.
- **Contabo** gives more RAM per euro but sits behind a longer setup and more manual PCI compliance.
- **DigitalOcean / Linode** remain strong for developer comfort, though the price/performance lags Hetzner slightly.

## What the comparison covers

## The alternatives I still use

- **Oracle Always Free** – best for zero-cost experiments. Add a 2GB swap, avoid Next.js builds without swap, and keep the ARM debugging story in mind.
- **Vultr High Frequency** – strong US east/LA nodes when latency matters. Accepts Wise and standard cards without weird 3DS issues.
- **Contabo VPS** – more RAM and disk for the same price, but slower provisioning and occasional payment delays.
- **Scaleway Start** – EU-friendly, works well with EU-based clients, especially when you need ARM64 or GPU options.
- **DigitalOcean / Linode** – developer-friendly dashboards; still worth it for teams that want pure simplicity even at slightly higher price.

## When to pivot away from Hetzner

- Billing fails repeatedly (3DS blockers) → try Wise or Oracle Free with pre-paid verification.
- You need a US node (Vultr, Linode, DigitalOcean) for local customers.
- You want more RAM than €5 can buy (Contabo or larger Hetzner plan).
- You need burstable GPU or ARM instances (Scaleway or Oracle A1).
