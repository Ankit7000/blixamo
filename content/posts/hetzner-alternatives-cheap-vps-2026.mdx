- **Hetzner is still the default** when you want strong price-per-spec and you are happy with its region footprint and account flow.

## What a real Hetzner alternative needs to beat

A "Hetzner alternative" should not mean "a random VPS company that is cheaper for one tiny plan." It should mean a provider you could realistically trust for a real project.

For me, that means checking five things:

1. **Starter price at the small-server tier**
2. **Region fit for your users**
3. **Storage and transfer at that price**
4. **Provisioning and account friction**
5. **How much platform comfort you get for the money**

Hetzner's current pricing benchmark is still hard to ignore. Its small cloud instances like the CPX12 and CPX22 are in the pricing band many solo developers compare against. That matters because it gives you a concrete baseline for judging every "alternative."

So the right question is not "who is cheaper than Hetzner?" The right question is: **who is better when Hetzner is not the right fit for this exact project?**

## 1) Oracle Cloud Free is the best zero-dollar fallback, not the easiest production host

Oracle Cloud's free tier is still unusually generous on paper. Oracle says its Always Free resources include Ampere A1 compute usage equivalent to 4 OCPUs and 24 GB of memory per month. That is why people keep trying it when they want to avoid paying for a VPS early.

The upside is obvious:

- zero monthly server cost for experiments
- enough free capacity on paper to run real test workloads
- useful for bots, dev environments, low-stakes staging, and learning

The downside is also obvious if you have actually used cheap or free infrastructure before: the more your project matters, the more "free" gets expensive in debugging time. ARM compatibility, allocation availability, and account friction matter more when you are trying to ship quickly.

So this is my honest framing: Oracle Cloud Free is great for **experiments, internal tools, and disposable workloads**. It is not my first recommendation for the one server you cannot afford to babysit.

## 2) Contabo is compelling when you care more about resources per euro than elegance

Contabo keeps making these shortlists for one reason: it offers a lot of server resources for the money. Its Cloud VPS 10 starts at around €4.50/month, which is exactly the kind of pricing that tempts cost-conscious builders comparing it with Hetzner or free-tier experiments.

This is the provider I would look at when your decision is dominated by:

- needing more RAM at the low end
- wanting larger storage without jumping into a much higher plan
- accepting a slightly less premium experience in exchange for raw value

But this is also where people make a common mistake. They compare only CPU and RAM columns, then act surprised when the day-to-day experience matters. Provider quality is not just specs. It is also:

- how predictable provisioning feels
- how easy snapshots and rebuilds feel
- how confident you are during incidents
- how clean the control panel and docs are when you are stressed

That is why I see Contabo as a **value-first pick**, not a universal Hetzner replacement.

## 3) Akamai Cloud is the clean "I want a real developer platform" alternative

Akamai Cloud, formerly Linode, is still one of the easiest names to recommend when someone wants a more globally familiar cloud VPS platform without jumping straight to hyperscaler complexity. Shared CPU instances start at $5 per month, and they are framed as a fit for development servers, low-traffic sites, staging, and general-purpose workloads.

That is why Akamai Cloud is a strong Hetzner alternative when:

- you want a mature, well-known developer platform
- you care about broad region availability
- you prefer a predictable shared CPU starter plan
- you want the experience to feel more "platform" and less "bargain hunt"

The trade-off is simple: you do not go there primarily to beat Hetzner on pure raw specs per euro. You go there because the overall package can be easier to trust for the right project.

If your project is client-facing, distributed, or likely to move beyond one tiny server, Akamai Cloud belongs near the top of the shortlist.

## 4) DigitalOcean is the easiest premium-feeling option, but the price gap becomes real

DigitalOcean remains the easiest recommendation for beginners who want a smooth UI, extensive docs, easy backups, and a platform that rarely feels confusing. The trade-off is price.

Basic Droplet pricing runs around $6/month for 1 GiB / 1 vCPU and $24/month for 4 GiB / 2 vCPUs. That is not outrageous, but it is materially more expensive than the cheapest value-first providers when you compare like-for-like small production boxes.

That is why DigitalOcean makes sense when:

- you value simplicity highly
- you want good documentation and onboarding
- you would rather pay a bit more to reduce early mistakes
- you are optimizing for speed of setup, not lowest monthly cost

If you are extremely budget-sensitive, you will feel the price jump sooner. If you value clean operations more than shaving a few dollars, DigitalOcean remains one of the best "just get me shipping" choices.

## 5) Vultr still matters when region flexibility beats perfect price efficiency

Vultr is still worth checking because the provider stays focused on globally available cloud compute and positionally competes on cost-conscious cloud hosting. Even when Hetzner wins the raw "cheap EU VPS" conversation, Vultr keeps showing up when the decision is more about region availability, deployment flexibility, or a specific location you need for latency or compliance reasons.

This is the main reason not to overfit your decision to one benchmark spreadsheet. Hosting is not just a spec problem. It is also a geography problem.

I would shortlist Vultr when:

- your users are not well served by Hetzner's strongest locations
- you want another mainstream developer cloud name in the mix
- you care about region choice more than absolute cheapest euro-per-GB value

When a project is global, region fit can matter more than a small monthly price difference.

## Mistakes people make when comparing cheap VPS providers

### Comparing list price only

A cheap VPS is not cheap if weak docs, bad fit, or slow debugging wastes your time every month.

### Treating free tier as production strategy

Free is a great way to learn. It is not automatically the best way to run something that matters.

### Ignoring region

A server that is "cheaper" but in the wrong place can create worse user experience than a slightly pricier box closer to your audience.

### Choosing a provider before choosing your stack

If you do not know whether you will run Node + PM2, Docker, a database, background jobs, or all four, your hosting decision will be half guesswork.

## FAQ

### Is there a provider cheaper than Hetzner?

Yes, depending on the exact plan and whether you mean free, lowest starter tier, or more RAM per euro. But "cheaper" is not enough. The real question is whether the provider gives you a better overall fit for your workload, region, and operational style.

### What is the best free Hetzner alternative?

For true zero-dollar compute, Oracle Cloud Free is the obvious answer. It is generous enough to be useful, but it is best treated as an experiment-friendly option first, not an automatic long-term production default.

### Which Hetzner alternative is best for beginners?

DigitalOcean is usually the easiest beginner choice if budget allows. Akamai Cloud is also strong if you want a more traditional developer cloud feel. Both trade some raw price efficiency for smoother onboarding.

### Which alternative is best for low-budget production?

If you need a real paid production box and want to stay cost-conscious, Contabo and Akamai Cloud are both worth serious consideration for different reasons. Contabo leans value-first. Akamai Cloud leans platform-first.

### Should I leave Hetzner just because another host is slightly cheaper?

Usually no. Switching makes sense when there is a concrete reason: region, account flow, free tier, resource profile, or team preference. Switching only for a tiny monthly difference is often false economy.

### Is cheap VPS hosting enough for production apps?

Yes, for many small and medium projects. But cheap hosting only works well when you pair it with sane deployment, backups, monitoring, and hardening. Cheap infrastructure without good operations is not actually cheap.
