## Why Indian Cards Fail on Developer Platforms

There are usually three separate problems:

### 1. RBI 3DS rules

Many Indian cards expect an OTP-based international flow. A lot of developer platforms do not handle that flow well, so the bank declines the transaction.

### 2. Recurring billing friction

The first payment may work and the renewal may still fail. This is why "it worked once" is not the same as "this is a reliable subscription card."

### 3. International transactions are disabled or unstable

Many cards ship with international usage disabled or limited. Even after you enable it, some banks still behave unpredictably for recurring SaaS billing.

## Troubleshooting

### The card fails immediately

Check international transactions first. If it is a standard domestic debit card, stop retrying and switch to Niyo or Wise.

### The first payment worked but renewal failed

That is the recurring-billing problem. Move the subscription to a stronger card before the next cycle.

### The platform added the card but still shows payment issues

Wait for the first verification charge to settle and check whether the platform actually accepted the billing method. If not, switch cards instead of repeating the same flow.
