## Why Indian Cards Fail on Dev Platforms

Before getting to what works, it helps to understand why Indian cards fail. There are three distinct problems:

**The RBI 3DS Mandate**
Every international transaction on Indian cards requires 3D Secure (3DS) authentication — an OTP sent to your phone. Most foreign developer platforms don't support 3DS. Their payment forms don't have a place for an OTP. The transaction gets submitted without it, and the bank declines it automatically.

**The e-Mandate Problem**
SaaS tools like GitHub Pro, AWS, and Vercel charge you on a recurring basis. Indian banks have strict RBI rules around e-mandates for recurring international charges. Banks like SBI block these by default. Even if your first payment goes through, the renewal gets declined.

**The International Transaction Block**
Many Indian bank accounts have international transactions disabled by default. You enable it in the app, it works once, then the bank's fraud detection flags the pattern and blocks subsequent charges.

The cards that work have found ways around all three of these problems — usually by operating as prepaid forex cards or through international banking infrastructure.

## The Cards — Ranked

### 1. Niyo Global (Best Overall)

Niyo Global is a zero-forex-markup prepaid card issued in partnership with SBM Bank India. It's designed specifically for international use — and it works on developer platforms because it doesn't go through the standard Indian banking rails that cause 3DS failures.

**What works with Niyo:**
- GitHub Pro / GitHub Copilot
- Hetzner Cloud
- DigitalOcean
- Netlify Pro
- Vercel Pro
- AWS (with some setup — see note below)
- Anthropic API / OpenAI API
- Namecheap / Porkbun domains
- Cloudflare

**Cost:** Zero annual fee. Zero forex markup (you pay the mid-market rate). Reload via UPI or NEFT.

**How to get it:**
1. Download the Niyo Global app (iOS / Android)
2. Complete KYC (Aadhaar + PAN — takes 10-15 minutes)
3. Your virtual card is active immediately. Physical card ships in 7-10 days.
4. Load money via UPI — minimum ₹100

**For Hetzner specifically:** I use Niyo for this. Works perfectly. Load ₹600, add the card to Hetzner, the ₹30 verification charge goes through, and monthly billing runs without issues. Full walkthrough in my [Hetzner payment guide](/blog/pay-hetzner-from-india).

**AWS note:** AWS has its own verification quirks. Niyo works for the initial setup but some users report the free tier verification charge failing. If it does, use Wise instead for AWS.

### 2. Kotak 811 Virtual Card (Best Free Option)

Kotak 811 is a zero-balance digital savings account. The virtual debit card it issues works surprisingly well for international developer subscriptions — better than most full-service bank cards.

**What works with Kotak 811:**
- GitHub Pro
- Namecheap / GoDaddy domains
- Cloudflare
- Most one-time charges on dev platforms
- DigitalOcean (initial charge)

**Cost:** Free account, free virtual card. Forex markup of around 3.5% applies.

**How to get it:**
1. Download the Kotak 811 app
2. Open account with Aadhaar + PAN (fully digital, 10 minutes)
3. Virtual card is in the app immediately
4. Add money via UPI

**Limitation:** Kotak 811 works well for one-time charges but recurring billing is less reliable. If a monthly renewal fails, log into the platform and manually retry — it usually goes through on the second attempt.

### 3. Wise Multi-Currency Card (Best for Large Bills)

Wise is not an Indian bank — it's a UK-based fintech that lets you hold and spend in multiple currencies. The Wise card is a Mastercard/Visa that works everywhere internationally, with no Indian banking restrictions at all.

**What works with Wise:**
- Everything. AWS, GCP, Azure, GitHub, Vercel, Hetzner, DigitalOcean, OpenAI, Anthropic — all of it.
- Large recurring bills (₹5,000+/month)
- Team subscriptions where your company reimburses you

**Cost:** One-time card issuance fee of around ₹600. Transfer fees are small (typically 0.4-0.6% of the amount). No annual fee after that.

**How to get it:**
1. Sign up at wise.com
2. Complete KYC (PAN + selfie)
3. Add money from your Indian bank account via IMPS/NEFT
4. Order the physical card (₹600 fee) or use virtual card immediately

**Why I recommend Wise for AWS:** AWS's payment system is particularly strict. Wise's international Mastercard bypasses every Indian banking restriction that causes AWS failures. If you're running production workloads on AWS and can't afford payment failures, Wise is worth the ₹600.

### 4. HDFC Millennia / Regalia Credit Card (If You Have One)

HDFC credit cards — particularly Millennia and Regalia — have the best international transaction success rates among traditional Indian bank cards. They support 3DS properly through HDFC's OTP system, and recurring charges work reliably once set up.

**Works on:**
- GitHub (credit cards only for some plans)
- AWS
- Most major platforms

**The catch:** You need a credit card, not a debit card. HDFC debit cards are inconsistent — credit cards are reliable. If you already have an HDFC credit card, try it first before getting a new card.

---

### 5. SBI Debit Card (Avoid for Dev Tools)

SBI is India's largest bank, but their international transaction infrastructure is the worst for developer use. The combination of strict e-mandate blocking and poor 3DS implementation means SBI debit cards fail on most dev platforms.

**What usually fails:**
- Hetzner (almost always)
- AWS (frequently)
- DigitalOcean (often)
- Any SaaS with recurring billing

**Workaround if you must use SBI:**
Enable international transactions in YONO app → Settings → Card Settings → International Transactions. Then try adding the card. Even with this enabled, recurring charges often fail. Use SBI only for one-time purchases, not subscriptions.

## Platform-Specific Guide

### GitHub Pro / GitHub Copilot

**Best card:** Niyo Global or Kotak 811

**Best card:** Wise
AWS is the most difficult platform for Indian developers. The ₹2 verification charge that AWS places on new cards fails on most Indian debit cards. Wise has the highest success rate. Niyo works for some users. HDFC credit card works.

## Troubleshooting

**Card added but payment fails immediately**
Usually a 3DS issue. Try Niyo or Wise instead — they bypass Indian 3DS requirements.

**First payment worked, renewal fails**
This is the e-mandate problem. Your bank approved the one-time charge but blocked the recurring setup. Switch to Wise for any subscription that bills monthly.

**"Your card does not support this type of purchase"**
Your card has international transactions disabled. For Kotak 811: Settings → Card Controls → International Transactions → Enable. For SBI: YONO app → Card Settings.

**Payment goes through but platform says "payment failed"**
The bank charged you but the platform didn't confirm. Check your bank statement and contact platform support with the transaction reference. This happens occasionally with Kotak 811 on some platforms.

**VPN interference**
Some platforms flag Indian IPs and add extra verification. If using a VPN, disable it during payment setup — use your real Indian IP. Add the card first, then use a VPN normally after.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which Indian debit card works best for GitHub subscriptions?**

Niyo Global is the most reliable for GitHub Pro and GitHub Copilot. Kotak 811 also works for most users. HDFC credit card is reliable if you have one. SBI debit card usually fails on GitHub's recurring billing.

**Can I use UPI to pay for international developer tools?**

No. UPI is a domestic Indian payment system and is not accepted by international platforms like AWS, GitHub, Hetzner, or Vercel. You need an international debit card, credit card, or PayPal.

**Is Niyo Global safe for online payments?**

Yes. Niyo is issued by SBM Bank India (regulated by RBI) and uses standard Visa/Mastercard security. You can freeze the card instantly from the app and set transaction limits. I've used it for 2+ years without any fraud issues.

**Does Wise work without a permanent address in India?**

Wise KYC requires PAN card and address proof. A rental agreement or utility bill works. You don't need a permanent address — a current address is sufficient.

**What is the best card for AWS free tier verification?**

Wise is the most reliable for AWS. The ₹2 verification charge that AWS places on new cards fails on most Indian debit cards but almost never fails on Wise. Niyo Global is the second-best option.

**Can I get a refund to my Niyo or Kotak 811 card?**

Yes. International refunds work on both cards. Niyo refunds typically credit within 5-7 business days. Kotak 811 refunds take 7-14 days. The forex rate applied may differ from the original charge rate.

**Does Kotak 811 charge annual fees?**

No. Kotak 811 is a zero-balance account with no annual fee and no minimum balance requirement. The virtual card is free. Forex markup of around 3.5% applies on international transactions.

**Which card has the lowest forex fees for developer subscriptions?**

Niyo Global has zero forex markup — you pay the mid-market rate. Wise charges around 0.4-0.6% which is the next lowest. All other Indian cards charge 3-3.5% forex markup plus a small cross-currency transaction fee.
