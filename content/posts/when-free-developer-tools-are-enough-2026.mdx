## Decision Framework

Use this page when you need a call, not a tour.

| Question | Lean toward A | Lean toward B |
|---|---|---|
| Do you optimize for speed right now? | Choose the simpler workflow that shortens feedback loops. | Choose the heavier option only if it removes a recurring bottleneck. |
| Do you need tighter control or lower lock-in? | Prefer local-first, exportable, predictable tools. | Prefer hosted or managed options when team overhead matters more. |
| Do you care about handoff and maintenance? | Pick the option a second person can understand quickly. | Avoid setups that only make sense to the person who built them. |

My bias: default to the path with fewer hidden dependencies, fewer moving pieces, and faster rollback. That usually gives solo builders and small teams a better long-term result than the flashy option.

## How to Keep Free Tools From Slowing You Down

Free tools help when they keep momentum high and decision cost low. They hurt when they push hidden work onto setup, exports, sharing, or maintenance. The sweet spot is simple: pick free tools where the workflow is single-player or lightly collaborative, where your data is exportable, and where you can replace the tool without a quarter-long migration. When the tool becomes part of an audit trail or critical shared process, paid often wins earlier than people want to admit.

## Watch Out For

Do not overvalue feature count. Weight exportability, recovery, and daily friction more heavily.
