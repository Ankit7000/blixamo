# Support Workflow for Solo SaaS Founders

Support operations are where a lot of solo products start leaking time. This piece is about support workflow for solo SaaS founders with a bias toward repeatability, guardrails, and surviving bad days without turning into a full-time support desk.

## TL;DR

- Treat **support workflow for solo SaaS founders** as an operational decision, not just a setup task.
- Keep the workflow small enough that you can still run it on a bad day without chaos.
- Use one proof step, one rollback path, and one post-change verification step before calling it done.

## Why This Matters

Support work becomes expensive when every request feels urgent and every reply has to be written from scratch. This article exists to keep triage, canned replies, escalation, and handoff simple enough for one person to run without losing the whole week to inbox noise. ## The Working Model

Here is the model I recommend for **support workflow for solo SaaS founders**:

- keep the first version small enough to test in one sitting
- prefer defaults that make rollback obvious
- add observability before scale forces you to
- document the exact failure signs you care about
- push complexity out until the simple version genuinely hurts

That model keeps the article practical and stops it from turning into another abstract best-practices page.

## How to Keep the Workflow Lean

The best indie workflows are the ones you can still run when a launch spike, customer bug, and billing question hit on the same day. That means short loops, clear defaults, and a system that creates signal instead of more inbox noise. You do not need a giant SaaS stack to do that. You need one place to collect issues, one habit for prioritizing them, and one operational boundary that stops every conversation from becoming an emergency.

## Watch Out For

Do not let every support request enter the same queue. Route billing, bug, and usage questions differently.
