# Coolify vs CapRover vs Dokku in 2026: Which one should you use?

I’ve tested enough VPS app setups to know the hard part is not getting one app online. It is choosing a platform you still like after your second deploy, third domain, fourth database, and first broken SSL setup.

If you want the quick answer, **Coolify is the best choice for most developers in 2026**. **Dokku** is still the smartest pick when you want the lightest setup possible. **CapRover** still works, but I would only choose it when you already like its workflow and want to stay with it.

<Callout type='info' title='TL;DR'>
Coolify is the best default pick because it gives the strongest balance of usability and control. Dokku wins when you want the lightest VPS footprint. CapRover still works, but it is now the third choice unless you already know and like its workflow.
</Callout>

## Quick verdict

- **Best for most developers:** Coolify
- **Best for lean VPS setups:** Dokku
- **Best if you already prefer its workflow:** CapRover

If your goal is to run multiple apps on one VPS without stitching together your own deploy, SSL, database, and backup workflow, start with **Coolify**.

## The short answer

Use **Coolify** if you want the best balance of control, convenience, and day-2 usability.

Use **Dokku** if you care more about low overhead than dashboards.

Use **CapRover** if you already know its model, like how it behaves, and do not feel a strong reason to switch.

That is the real answer. The rest of this article is about why those recommendations hold up from different angles.

## Why this choice matters more in 2026

In 2026, self-hosting is not only about saving money. It is about owning the deployment workflow, avoiding platform lock-in, and making one decent VPS do more work.

The wrong platform creates drag fast. A good one should help you:

- deploy from Git without friction
- handle domains and SSL without weird hacks
- keep databases easy to attach and manage
- run multiple apps on one server without chaos
- stay manageable when your stack grows

That is why this is not really a “which tool is coolest” article. It is a “which platform wastes the least of your time after month 1” article.

## Coolify vs CapRover vs Dokku on setup friction

This is the first filter, because most people overestimate how much setup pain they are willing to tolerate.

**Coolify** is not the lightest option, but it usually gives the cleanest path to a proper app-hosting setup. You connect a server, bring in repos, add domains, provision databases, and manage apps from one place. The appeal is not just ease on day 1. It is that the setup work keeps paying you back later.

**CapRover** can still get apps online quickly, but the workflow feels more opinionated and a bit less compelling once you compare it directly with what Coolify now gives you.

**Dokku** is leaner by design. If you are comfortable with SSH, plugins, and working close to raw server behavior, Dokku feels refreshingly direct. The tradeoff is simple: more of the setup stays your job.

My read:

- **Fastest path to a polished control layer:** Coolify
- **Fastest path to a lean deployable base:** Dokku
- **Acceptable middle option:** CapRover

### Scenario: You are running 3 client apps on one VPS

This is where **Coolify** starts to pull away. When domains, databases, deploy history, and app-level settings all live inside one cleaner management layer, the platform saves time every week instead of only feeling nice on install day.

## Which one feels better on a small VPS?

If you are on a smaller VPS and every bit of RAM matters, **Dokku** is the safest answer. It keeps the platform layer lighter and does not try to be a broad visual control plane for everything.

**CapRover** sits in the middle. It is not as lean as Dokku, but it is also not trying to be as expansive as Coolify.

**Coolify** is usually worth the added overhead when you actually use what it gives you: better app management, easier database handling, stronger visibility across projects, and a more comfortable multi-app workflow. But if you are running one or two tiny apps on a very constrained box, Dokku’s lighter footprint is hard to ignore.

My rule is simple:

- choose **Dokku** for the leanest server setups
- choose **Coolify** when usability matters more than squeezing every bit of overhead
- choose **CapRover** only if you already know you want its style

## Best for beginners

For beginners, **Coolify** is the best option.

That does not mean self-hosting suddenly becomes easy. It means Coolify removes more of the glue work that usually makes beginners hate VPS deployment in the first place.

A beginner-friendly platform should reduce pain around:

- reverse proxy setup
- SSL wiring
- domains across multiple apps
- app-plus-database relationships
- keeping track of what is running where

Coolify handles more of that in one place. That lowers mental load, which matters more than marketing promises about simplicity.

CapRover is still approachable, but it no longer feels like the most convincing first recommendation. Dokku can work for beginners with strong CLI confidence, but I would not call it the best beginner choice overall.

## Best for control

For raw control, **Dokku** still has the clearest case.

It keeps you closer to the terminal, closer to the server, and closer to how the deployment actually behaves. Some people hate that. Some people specifically want that.

If your ideal platform means:

- fewer layers between you and the server
- more terminal-first workflows
- less dashboard dependency
- more explicit control over how apps are assembled

then Dokku makes sense.

Coolify gives you plenty of control too, but through a more managed experience. That is exactly why it is better for most people. If you define control as directness, Dokku wins.

### Scenario: You want the lightest setup possible

Pick **Dokku**. If your main goal is “give me the fewest extra layers and let me handle the rest,” it is the best fit of the three.

## Best for low-overhead VPS setups

This is the easiest call in the article.

For low-overhead VPS setups, **Dokku is the winner**.

That makes Dokku a good fit for:

- tiny production apps
- personal projects
- early SaaS prototypes
- budget VPS setups
- developers who do not mind doing more manually

Coolify can still run well on sensible VPS plans, but it makes the most sense when you want a fuller platform experience. CapRover is workable, but it is not the clear winner on footprint.

So if your main question is, “Which one feels least wasteful on a smaller VPS?” the answer is **Dokku**.

Pick Coolify when these matter more than absolute minimalism:

- cleaner app management
- easier multi-app hosting

- easier database handling

This is why Coolify wins for most developers. Not because it is the lightest. Because it gives the best tradeoff between convenience and control.

## When CapRover is the better choice

CapRover is the better choice when you already like its model and want to stay with a familiar panel-driven workflow without moving all the way to Coolify or all the way down to Dokku.

That is a narrower recommendation than it used to be, but it is still real.

CapRover can make sense if:

- you already know its workflow
- you want something visual without going fully terminal-first
- your current deployment pattern already fits how CapRover organizes apps
- your team is comfortable with its tradeoffs

What I would not do is recommend CapRover as the default winner for new readers comparing these three from scratch. That spot belongs to Coolify now.

### Scenario: You already know CapRover and your team is fine with it

Stay with **CapRover** unless you have a clear migration reason. If the current workflow is stable, predictable, and your team moves fast inside it, familiarity still has real value.

## SSL, domains, databases, and multi-app workflow

This is where the comparison stops being theoretical.

### SSL and domain handling

**Coolify** usually feels smoother when you are managing multiple apps and domains because the workflow is built for that reality.

**CapRover** still gets the job done, but it feels more opinionated in how it wants the setup to behave.

**Dokku** can absolutely handle domains and SSL, but it asks you to be more comfortable with the plumbing. That is fine for CLI-first users. It is not ideal for people trying to reduce operational friction.

### Database workflow

Coolify has the best overall story here for most people. Managing app-plus-database relationships feels more natural when the moving parts sit inside one cleaner operating surface.

With Dokku, you can get the control you want, but the experience is more manual. With CapRover, it works, but it is not where the platform feels strongest.

### Multi-app workflow

This is one of the biggest reasons Coolify gets the overall win. Once you host multiple projects, the quality of the management layer matters more than it did on day 1.

## FAQ

### Is Dokku still worth using in 2026?

Yes. Dokku is still worth using in 2026 if you want a lightweight, CLI-first deployment setup. It is not the best fit for everyone, but it is still a smart pick for developers who value low overhead and direct control more than a polished dashboard.

### Does CapRover still make sense for small teams?

Yes. CapRover can still make sense for small teams, especially when they already know its workflow and want a visual deployment layer without going fully terminal-first. I just would not call it the default recommendation when Coolify now covers the “easy but powerful” position better.

### Which one is best for a small VPS?

For a small VPS, Dokku is usually the best fit. It keeps the platform layer lighter and avoids some of the extra overhead that comes with a broader management experience. Coolify can still be worth it if usability matters more than squeezing every bit of RAM.

### Is Coolify better than CapRover?

For most developers in 2026, yes. Coolify is the better choice because it gives a stronger overall experience for multi-app hosting, databases, domains, and day-to-day management. CapRover still works, but it is harder to recommend as the first pick now.

### Should beginners use Dokku?

Only if they are already comfortable with the terminal and do not mind a more hands-on setup. For most beginners, Coolify is the safer recommendation because it reduces the amount of infrastructure glue work they need to manage themselves.

### Can I migrate from CapRover to Coolify?

Yes, but treat it like a controlled migration, not a casual swap. The exact work depends on how your apps, domains, volumes, and databases are currently wired. If your team already runs smoothly on CapRover, stay there until you have a real reason to move. If the current workflow is creating friction, migrating to Coolify makes more sense once you want better visibility across multiple apps.

### Which one is easiest for a solo developer shipping multiple projects?

For most solo developers, Coolify is the easiest option once the server hosts more than one real project. Dokku stays attractive when you want the lightest setup and you are happy doing more in the terminal. CapRover is easier to justify when you already know it, but it is harder to recommend as the first choice for a fresh three-way decision.
