## Where AWS Still Wins (Honest Answer)

I'm not going to pretend Hetzner is better in every situation. Here is where AWS is genuinely the right choice:

**Use AWS when:**
- You need autoscaling — traffic spikes from 100 to 10,000 users in minutes
- You need managed services like RDS Multi-AZ, ElastiCache, SQS
- Your company requires SOC 2 / HIPAA / enterprise compliance
- You're building in a team and need IAM, VPCs, fine-grained permissions
- You need US-east or US-west latency under 20ms

**Use Hetzner when:**
- You're an indie developer or small startup under 10K daily users
- Your traffic is predictable and doesn't spike 100x overnight
- You want to actually understand your infrastructure
- You want zero surprise bills
- You're comfortable with SSH and basic Linux

The honest answer: 90% of side projects and early-stage startups do not need AWS. They use it because it's what they learned, not because it's the right tool.

## Troubleshooting: 3 Things That Surprised Me

### PostgreSQL connections maxed out

Self-hosted Postgres has lower `max_connections` than RDS by default. Edit `/etc/postgresql/16/main/postgresql.conf` and increase `max_connections = 200`. Restart Postgres. Most apps need 50-100 connections max — the default 100 is fine for most projects.

### Disk I/O slower than RDS on heavy queries

RDS uses optimized EBS storage. Hetzner uses NVMe SSD which is fast, but for very query-heavy workloads RDS Multi-AZ is genuinely better. For most CRUD apps: no difference.

### SSH access from India

Hetzner VPS is in Europe. SSH from India adds ~150ms latency to your terminal commands — you barely notice it. If it bothers you, use VSCode Remote SSH which caches the filesystem locally.
